iPhone Touch Screen Not Working (Fix It Now)

Updated Feb 13, 2026

⚠️ Immediate Fix

Ghost touch or unresponsive screen? Don't panic. This is usually static buildup or a software glitch, not a broken screen. Clean your screen thoroughly with a cloth, remove any case, and perform a Force Restart (Vol Up, Vol Down, Hold Power) to reset the digitizer calibration.

Why This Happens

  • Static Buildup: Electricity can confuse the capacitive sensors.
  • Screen Protector: Cheap protectors warp and press incorrectly.
  • Software Crash: The touch driver service has frozen.
  • Storage Full: System cannot process touch inputs.

Step-by-Step Fix

  1. Clean and Free the Screen: Remove your case and screen protector. Wipe the screen vigorously with a microfiber cloth to discharge static.
  2. Force Restart (The Reset): Press Volume Up, then Volume Down, then HOLD the Side Button. Keep holding until the Apple Logo appears. This reboots the touch drivers.
  3. disconnect Accessories: Unplug any Lightning/USB-C cables or headphones. Faulty cables can cause "grounding" issues that disable touch.
  4. Update via Computer: If you can't touch to update, plug into a PC/Mac. Open iTunes/Finder and select "Update" to reinstall iOS without data loss.

When to Stop

If you see physical cracks, black ink blobs (pixel death), or colored lines, the digitizer is physically broken. No software fix will help. You need a screen replacement from Apple or a repair shop.
